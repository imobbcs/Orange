# Bitcoin for Beginners: 5 Common Mistakes to Avoid in Your First Year

**Disclaimer:** This article is for informational purposes only and does not constitute financial advice.

## Introduction

Starting with Bitcoin is exciting — but it's easy to stumble into common pitfalls. In this guide, we'll walk you through five frequent beginner mistakes, how to avoid them, and how to set yourself up for success.

---

## Mistake 1: Overtrading

Many beginners try to "time the market," constantly buying and selling based on short-term price movements. This often leads to:
- High transaction fees eating into gains.
- Emotional decisions rather than rational strategies.
- Missing out on long-term growth by panicking during dips.

**Tip:** Focus on long-term strategies like holding or dollar-cost averaging (DCA), which reduce the need for constant trades.

---

## Mistake 2: Poor Security Practices

Crypto is a self-custody world — if you lose access, there's no "forgot password" button. Common security mistakes include:
- Using weak passwords.
- Skipping two-factor authentication (2FA).
- Not backing up your wallet seed phrase.
- Storing coins on exchanges indefinitely.

**Tip:** Learn how to set up a hardware or software wallet and practice self-custody basics. Tools like [Satoshi Assistant](https://whentobuybtc.xyz) offer beginner-friendly security resources.

---

## Mistake 3: Leaving Coins on Exchanges

Exchanges can be hacked or go bankrupt. "Not your keys, not your coins" means that unless you control the private keys, you don't truly own your Bitcoin.

**Tip:** Withdraw your holdings to a wallet you control.

---

## Mistake 4: Falling for Hype or Scams

From pump-and-dump schemes to phishing emails, the crypto space is full of bad actors. Be wary of:
- Influencers promising guaranteed returns.
- Projects asking for your private keys.
- Clicking on suspicious links or downloads.

**Tip:** Stick to reputable resources like [CoinGecko](https://coingecko.com) or [CoinMarketCap](https://coinmarketcap.com) and always double-check URLs.

---

## Mistake 5: Ignoring Fees

Bitcoin transactions aren't free — miners are paid through fees. Beginners often:
- Overpay by using unnecessarily high fee settings.
- Get stuck with slow confirmations by underpaying.

**Tip:** Learn how to use dynamic fee estimators and set appropriate fees for your needs.
